# What's Xscribe?

Xscribe (transcribe) is based on my personal shorthand that borrows kanji (Chinese characters) or glyphs without linguistic accuracy. 

Main purpose: Take the shape and meaning of the characters to reinforce a concept. 

**This shorthand system doesn't obey Japanese grammatical rules (like Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) agreement) for sentence structure.** 

Rather, kanji serve as an anchor for semantic domains (e.g. InfoSec, perspective drawing, etc.). 

Verbs at the end of sentences in English (Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) language) may not fit within the margin of my notebook and are sometimes replaced with Japanese verbs. 

The focus is mostly on kanji replacing lengthy or dense terms. 

My goal with this project is to automate or improve how I implement shorthand into my digital notes. For example, making current event articles in Cybersecurity more memorable for future conversations or notes, simplifying my notes on music theory for faster recall, and distinguishing the nuances of synonyms I encounter during my note-taking process.
